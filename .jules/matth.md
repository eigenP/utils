## 2024-05-24 - [Analytic Lineage Coupling via Hypergeometric Probabilities]
**Learning:** Lineage coupling (co-occurrence of cell types within clones) was previously calculated using a computationally expensive and non-deterministic permutation test. We found that the probability of specific types occurring in a clone of a given size under random assignment can be modeled exactly using hypergeometric/binomial statistics and the inclusion-exclusion principle. This exact statistical formulation eliminates stochastic variation, allows scaling to massive datasets, and avoids numerical edge cases of permutation tests by natively solving for exact means and variances in $O(N_{types}^2 \cdot N_{clones})$ time instead of $O(n\_permutations \cdot N_{clones})$.
**Action:** Always prefer analytic or exact probabilistic formulations (e.g., using properties of independent Bernoulli trials or hypergeometric statistics) over Monte-Carlo or permutation tests for simple combinatorial intersection/enrichment problems.
