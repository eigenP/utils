## 2025-05-21 - Hierarchical Dispersion Fallbacks for Zero-Inflated Distributions
**Learning:** Robust scaling using pure Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) critically fails (collapses to 0) on zero-inflated or highly-tied datasets where the dominant value represents >50% of the mass. Blindly regularizing MAD with unprincipled epsilon divisions (e.g., `mad + 1e-8`) obscures structural ties and artificially explodes the magnitude of outlier deviations, masking underlying statistical realities. Mathematical rigor requires a hierarchical fallback mechanism: when MAD collapses, it must dynamically fall back to Mean Absolute Deviation (MeanAD), intrinsically coupling the new location estimator (mean) to the new scale estimator.
**Action:** Always implement a hierarchical dispersion fallback (MAD -> MeanAD -> STD) when robustly scaling zero-inflated or tied distributions. Standard property-based tests must incorporate a "highly-tied" edge case (e.g., `[5, 5, 5, 5, 10, 20]`) to prove the fallback triggers successfully and preserves scaling geometry without epsilon padding.
