### Goal
Enhance the statistical correctness of `eigenp_utils/stats.py` by implementing the **Bias-Corrected and Accelerated (BCa)** bootstrap method.

### Why BCa?
Currently, the `bootstrap_ci` function implements standard Percentile and Bias-Corrected (BC) bootstrap methods. While BC corrects for median bias, it assumes that the standard error of the estimator is constant across different true values of the parameter (constant variance). This is often violated in practice (e.g., when the variance is related to the mean, like with right-skewed data or correlation coefficients).

The **BCa (Bias-Corrected and Accelerated)** method introduces an "acceleration factor" ($a$) that corrects for the skewness in the distribution of the estimator and allows the standard error to vary with the parameter. This makes it a second-order accurate method ($O(n^{-1})$ error vs. $O(n^{-1/2})$ for standard methods) and the gold standard for nonparametric bootstrap confidence intervals.

By replacing 'bc' with 'bca' or adding 'bca', we improve the robustness and theoretical soundness of all confidence intervals generated by this library.
